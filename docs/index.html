Pen Facts

A. T. Cross

The company is named after Alonzo Townsend Cross, born in 1846, in Birmingham, England.

Cross launched its first ballpoint pen in 1952. The ballpoint was so immediately successful that Cross stopped making fountain pens entirely. Cross did not produce another fountain pen until 1982, when it launched the Century.

In 1972, the company went public on the American Stock Exchange

Aurora

Fabbrica Italiana de Penne a Serbatoio-Aurora was founded in Torino in 1919. One of Aurora's competitive strengths was in its colourful barrels (compared to most fountain pens which were monochromatic during this era). In 1925 Aurora began producing pens with vivid celluloid barrels.

In 1943, Aurora's entire facility was destroyed during the bombing of Turin.

After the war's end, many Americans were seen with the radical-looking Parker 51 which inspired Aurora to produce the Aurora 88, which it launched in 1947 and has been its most well-known model ever since.

Montblanc

August Eberstein founded the Simplizissimus-Füllhalter company in Berlin in 1906.

In 1910, the company started putting a white tip on the end of its pen caps. By 1914, this tip had morphed into the trademarkable 'star' that has characterised Montblancs ever since. Although known as a star, the six-pointed design element actually represents the snow-covered tip of Mont Blanc

In 1924, Montblanc introduced the Meisterstück branding.

Nakaya

The Nakaya Fountain Pen Company was formed by Platinum president Toshiya Nakata in 2000, together with several retired Platinum craftsmen.

In 2005, Nakaya extended their use of Maki-e by creating decorated converters.

In 2006, they released the Decapod, a ten-faced ebonite pen.

Parker

George Safford Parker was born in 1863.

In 1898, Parker obtained the first patent on the slip-fit type of outer cap.

In 1904, the Company obtained a patent on a mechanically-filled pen.

In 1921, Parker introduce the duofold, available in bright red.

One of the most successful fountain pens of all time, the Parker 51 was launched in 1941. Parker's first cartridge pen, the Parker 45 came out in 1960.
